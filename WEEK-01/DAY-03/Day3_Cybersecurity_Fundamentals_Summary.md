# Day 3 – Cybersecurity Fundamentals

## Overview
In this session I reflected on the core building blocks every security professional should master. I organized the concepts into a mental roadmap that will guide my work on the Threat Intelligence Platform.

## Key Topics Covered

1. **Why Cybersecurity Matters**
   - The field is vast; rushing the learning process leads to gaps.
   - Emphasis on a structured, layered approach.

2. **Security Frameworks & Standards**
   - Familiarity with established frameworks (NIST, SANS, ISO 27001) provides a common language for risk management and compliance.
   - Mapping controls to these frameworks helps ensure the platform meets industry requirements.

3. **Identity & Access Management**
   - Distinguish **authentication** (verifying identity) from **authorization** (granting permissions).
   - Importance of preventing privilege creep by regularly reviewing role assignments and using least‑privilege principles.

4. **Network Security Basics**
   - Role of firewalls, IDS vs. IPS, and proper segmentation.
   - Understanding how traffic filtering and monitoring protect the data plane of the TIP.

5. **Vulnerability Management**
   - Process of discovering, assessing, prioritizing, and remediating weaknesses.
   - Integration of vulnerability feeds into the threat intelligence pipeline.

6. **Operating‑System Knowledge**
   - Deep familiarity with Windows domain environments and Linux host hardening is essential for effective incident response.

7. **Incident Response Lifecycle**
   - Preparation, detection, containment, eradication, recovery, and post‑incident lessons learned.
   - Building playbooks that tie directly into the dynamic policy enforcer.

## Goal of the Session
To cement a structured mental model of the cybersecurity landscape so that I can design, implement, and continuously improve the TIP and its automated enforcement mechanisms.
