# Day 1 – Introduction to Cybersecurity

## Overview
In this opening session, I explored the core concepts of cybersecurity and reflected on why protecting data, devices, and networks is crucial in today’s interconnected world.

## Key Topics Covered

1. **What is Cybersecurity?**
   - Definition: protection of data, devices, networks, and services from unauthorized access, disruption, or theft.
   - Emphasis on the growing need for security as more devices become connected.

2. **The CIA Triad**
   - **Confidentiality** – ensuring only authorized users can view information.
   - **Integrity** – maintaining the accuracy and consistency of data, preventing unauthorized changes.
   - **Availability** – guaranteeing that systems and data are accessible to authorized users when needed.

3. **Risk Management Basics**
   - Identifying **vulnerabilities** (weaknesses) and **threats** (potential attacks).
   - Assessing risk and deciding on mitigation strategies.

4. **Cybersecurity Landscape**
   - Overview of common attack types and how they have evolved.
   - Introductory look at roles in the field (e.g., ethical hacking, security analyst).

## Goal of the Session
To demystify cybersecurity for beginners, provide a clear mental model (the CIA Triad), and set the foundation for deeper topics such as cryptography, network security, and operating‑system hardening.
